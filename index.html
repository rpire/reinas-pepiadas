The Reina Pepiada

About It

The arepa is the venezuelan's everyday dish, it has always been one of the most popular dishes in the venezuelan's menu and the most beloved meal in this caribbean country. Among all the different kinds and flavors of arepas, there is one that fills my heart completely, that kind of arepa is the beautiful and magnificent Reina Pepiada.

The Reina Pepiada is an arepa stuffed with a mix of chicken, avocado and mayonnaise. It's creators were the Álvarez brothers in the decade of the 50's. It has its name in honor of that year's beauty queen; Susana Duijim, that year's winner of the Miss World beauty contest.

Susana Duijim wearing the Miss World Crown

Ingredients

Although it is an extremely delicious meal, the ingredients for it can be found quite easily. You can find the ingredients calculated for three (3) serving down here:

  • 1 1/2 cups of cornmeal
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1 avocado
  • 1/2 chicken breast (cooked)
  • 1 onion
  • 5 tbsp of mayonnaise
  • 2 oz cilantro
  • 1 tbsp of olive oil
  • 1 tbsp of salt
  • 1/2 tsp pepper

Preparation

Step 1/7

  1. Shredd the chicken breast completely
  2. Finely chop the onion and cilantro, and dice the avocado

Step 2/7

Mix all ingredients well in a bowl, add the mayonnaise and season to taste with salt and pepper.

Step 3/7

Stir water and a pinch of salt together in a bowl until salt dissolves. Then gradually add the cornmeal, mixing with your hands to dissolve any lumps, adding enough to make a soft dough that holds its shape without cracking when molded.

Step 4/7

Divide the dough into 3 pieces. To give arepas its shape, roll the dough into a ball and then pat it between your hands into a disc.

Press your thumbs around the outside of the disk to form a nice edge.

Step 5/7

Put a small amount of oil on a large iron or a grill pan over medium heat. Let the skillet get hot before adding the arepa.

Cook the arepas for about 5 minutes on each side or until they start getting light brown spots.

Step 6/7

Once the arepas are ready, let them cool for a few minutes before handling. Carefully slice the arepas way through, creating a pocket. Fill with the previously prepared filling in equal quantities.

Step 7/7

Enjoy your meal :).
